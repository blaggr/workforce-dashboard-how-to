Preparing for implementation

Step · Form your team

Assemble a small, cross-functional team

Four perspectives keep a dashboard honest and used. Tap each to see what they own.

+

IT / Data lead

Connects the systems
Pulls the data and keeps the pipes flowing, so each visual is fed by a reliable, current source.
+

HR representative

Frames the questions
Brings the workforce questions that matter most and helps define what each metric should mean.
+

Frontline supervisor

The end-user
Tests whether the dashboard answers real questions from the field. Build for this person.
+

Leadership

The sponsor
Clears barriers and signals that the work matters. If they cannot join every meeting, keep them informed to support buy-in.

Step · Data readiness assessment

Review your existing data sources

Know what you already hold before you design anything. Open each group to review the usual suspects.

Core workforce sources
  • HR systems
  • SACWIS / CCWIS (state child-welfare information systems)
  • Payroll systems
  • Caseload assignment and workflow data
  • Training and program enrollment / attendance logs
Program & services sources
  • Assessment results
  • Participant engagement and satisfaction surveys
  • Referral logs
  • Supportive-services disbursements
  • Job placement and employer-engagement logs

Then check the data itself

Tick each as you confirm it. Data should be:

  • Clean — consistent, deduplicated, few gaps
  • Accessible — you can actually pull it when needed
  • Updated frequently — fresh enough to trust
Learn more: data quality (QIC-WD resource)

Other workforce needs

Map your stakeholders and beneficiaries

+

What questions do they need answered?

Concrete steps

The implementation roadmap

Eight steps, start to finish. Tap any step to jump to it, or move through them in order.

01Step 1 of 8
Define

Define your Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Focus on a narrow set of workforce metrics. A few sharp numbers beat a wall of charts.

Vacancy rate by officeAverage caseload per worker90-day turnover

Each metric should tie back to the “why” you named earlier.

Metric
Office vacancy rate
30 vacant of 120 positions = 25%
Calculated KPI
Vacancy vs. target
25% against a 15% goal = +10 pts to watch
Learn more: workforce metrics (QIC-WD resource)
02Step 2 of 8
Map

Map fields to each visual

For every visual, identify exactly which database fields feed it, and how those fields relate.

Vacancy rate

Positions table + a “filled” flag, grouped by office.

Average caseload

Open cases ÷ active workers, from assignment data.

90-day turnover

Hire date and exit date, filtered to the first 90 days.

Mapping now prevents the “where does this number come from?” problem later.

07Step 7 of 8
Pilot

Pilot with a small group

Test with about five stakeholders. A good mix: HR, a child welfare director, an administrator, a supervisor, and a quality-assurance / continuous-quality-improvement manager.

What to watch during the pilot

  • Accessibility for every user
  • An intuitive layout that uses pre-attentive cues (color, size, position)
  • Ease and accuracy of reading the data
  • The actions users decide to take from it
  • Questions or needs that still feel unanswered, and how to fix them

Tick each as you confirm it with real users. Their confusion is your to-do list.

08Step 8 of 8
Evaluate

Evaluate the impact after launch

Launch is the start of learning, not the finish line. Plan to measure what changed.

Survey users at 3 to 6 months

Ask about use and impact once the dashboard has settled in.

Keep a feedback loop open

Give users ongoing ways to flag what to improve.

Track resource allocation

Did decisions about staffing and support actually shift?

Watch how staff are supported

The real test is whether frontline support improves.
